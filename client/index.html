📖 ORIENTEERING MANUAL

1. Voxel Wilderness Navigation

Welcome to Webteering! Orienteering is a tactical sport where you must navigate to a series of checkpoints (flags) in the exact numerical sequence using only a topographic map and a magnetic compass.

Move with WASD, look with your Mouse, and press E to punch checkpoints when standing near them.

2. The Topographic Map (ISOM Legends)

The map uses standard international orienteering color codes:

Yellow Fields
Open terrain, fast running.
White Forest
Clean runnable forest.
Light Green
Light foliage, slower.
Medium Green
Dense forest, heavy drag.
Blue Lakes
Deep water, swim speed.
Brown Paths
Gravel path, speed boost!

Contour Lines: The brown loops represent changes in elevation. The closer the loops are to each other, the steeper the voxel hill slope!

3. Realism Controls & Compass

Compass Needle Wobble: Running fast or jumping causes the magnetic needle to oscillate and swing violently due to lateral physical momentum. To take precise bearings, you must **slow down to a walk or stand still**!

Map Speed Penalty: Reading a map while running limits your focus. Opening the map panel (M key) applies an immediate **15% speed penalty**.

Map Rotation Modes:

  • North-Up: Map remains static.
  • Heading-Up: Map automatically rotates in reverse to match your camera direction.
  • Manual Mode: Use Q and R keys to rotate the map sheet manually in your hands!

4. E-Card Punching & Splits

Checkpoints are marked as red circles on the map. Stand within 3 meters and press E to stamp your electronic SportIdent e-card. Out-of-order punches trigger an error buzzer. Your splits and post-race paths are tracked for final review!
